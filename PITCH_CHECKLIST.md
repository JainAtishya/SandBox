# BrandMind AI - Pitch Preparation Checklist

## Pre-Pitch Setup (Do This First)

### [ ] Tech Setup (2-3 days before)
- [ ] Test your prototype on the presentation laptop/projector
- [ ] Ensure backend is stable (no timeouts on generation)
- [ ] Have a backup screenshot of generated websites (in case tech fails)
- [ ] Test all demo flows: input → generate → show both brands → edit tokens
- [ ] Have Wi-Fi backup (hotspot) in case venue internet is slow
- [ ] Reduce window clutter (close Slack, email, etc.)
- [ ] Use a clean, dark background theme (increases contrast)

### [ ] Slide Setup
- [ ] Create your one-page pitch slide (use guide above)
- [ ] Export as PDF + PowerPoint (backup formats)
- [ ] Test on projector for 2+ minutes
- [ ] Ensure all text is readable from 30 feet
- [ ] No animations (they distract from narrative)
- [ ] Have a backup slide with key talking points (if tech fails)

### [ ] Practice (1 week minimum)
- [ ] Run through the pitch 5+ times aloud
- [ ] Time yourself (target: 4:30-5:00 for main pitch, 1:00-1:30 for demo)
- [ ] Practice with someone unfamiliar with your project (get feedback)
- [ ] Anticipate questions and prepare 2-3 minute answers
- [ ] Practice what you'll say if the demo breaks
- [ ] Record yourself and watch it (you'll catch nervous habits)

## Day of Pitch: 1 Hour Before

### [ ] Final Checks
- [ ] Phone on silent
- [ ] Laptop fully charged (have charger nearby)
- [ ] Website loaded in browser (not starting fresh)
- [ ] Backup screenshots open in another tab
- [ ] Slide displayed (one click to start)
- [ ] Presentation notes printed (not reading from phone)

### [ ] Mental Prep
- [ ] Reread the closer: "Every tool generates a website. We generate *their* website."
- [ ] Remind yourself: You've built something real. You're not selling AI. You're proving you understood the problem.
- [ ] Take 3 deep breaths
- [ ] Remember: confidence comes from knowing your material, not perfection

## During Presentation: The Flow

### Phase 1: Setup (0:00-0:30)
- [ ] Walk to podium/stand confidently
- [ ] Make eye contact
- [ ] Thank judges
- [ ] Say: "Let me show you what's broken and how we fix it."
- [ ] Click to your pitch slide

### Phase 2: Problem (0:30-1:00)
- [ ] Read/explain the left section of slide
- [ ] Sound like you understand the problem deeply (because you do)
- [ ] Don't apologize for existing tools — just state facts
- [ ] Pause and let it land

### Phase 3: Approach (1:00-1:30)
- [ ] Explain the center section
- [ ] Say: "SLDS 2 is the grammar. Brand tokens are the voice. AI is the writer. You are the editor."
- [ ] Make it sound simple (it is)
- [ ] Connect to the core thesis: personality-driven design

### Phase 4: Demo (1:30-4:30)
- [ ] [Follow DEMO_SCRIPT.md beat-by-beat]
- [ ] Brand #1: input → generate → show
- [ ] Brand #2: same components, different feel
- [ ] Token tuning: show real-time responsiveness
- [ ] Always narrate (no silent moments)

### Phase 5: Close (4:30-5:00)
- [ ] Step back from screen
- [ ] Make eye contact
- [ ] Deliver your closer with conviction
- [ ] Pause for 2 seconds
- [ ] Say: "That's the product. Thank you."

### Phase 6: Questions (5:00+)
- [ ] Listen fully before answering
- [ ] Take 2 seconds to think (don't rush)
- [ ] Answer confidently (if you don't know, say "great question, here's my thinking...")
- [ ] Tie answers back to the five criteria if possible
- [ ] Never say "I don't know" — say "that's something we'd validate with..."

## Key Moments That Win or Lose Points

### ✅ Win These Moments
- [ ] **First impression (0-30 sec):** Judges immediately get the problem
- [ ] **Brand Intelligence (2:00-2:30):** Second brand looks completely different from first
- [ ] **Human Control (3:00-3:30):** Token edit updates the whole site in real-time
- [ ] **The Closer (4:50-5:00):** Delivers with confidence, sounds true, lands emotionally
- [ ] **Response to questions:** You sound like you've thought deeply, not just rehearsed

### ❌ Lose Points On These
- [ ] Spending >2 minutes on "what's wrong with existing tools"
- [ ] Not showing the second brand (they don't believe system extensibility)
- [ ] Second brand looking too similar to first (defeats the proof)
- [ ] Boring tone (makes even good product sound mediocre)
- [ ] Defensive answer when questioned (shows you haven't thought it through)
- [ ] Tech failure + panic (always have a backup)

## Scoring Breakdown (How to Maximize Points)

### Brand Intelligence (25%)
- **Win by:** Showing two brands using the same components but looking distinct
- **What to emphasize:** "Notice the personality is completely different"
- **Demo time:** 2:15-2:50 (second brand generation)

### System Extensibility (25%)
- **Win by:** Explaining SLDS 2 as the engine, showing token consistency
- **What to emphasize:** "Same structure, different tokens, credible output"
- **Demo time:** Highlight throughout (same components reused)

### Human-AI Collaboration (20%)
- **Win by:** Showing real-time token editing, explaining review flow
- **What to emphasize:** "The human steers. Every choice is visible."
- **Demo time:** 3:00-3:30 (token tuning)

### Output Quality (15%)
- **Win by:** Comparing generated website to Wix/Squarespace
- **What to emphasize:** "This looks professional. Not 'good for AI.'"
- **Demo time:** When website first loads (pause and let them absorb)

### Ease of Creation (15%)
- **Win by:** Quick input (<2 min) + instant output + non-technical
- **What to emphasize:** "Non-designer, 10 minutes, proud of result"
- **Demo time:** Input section (0:20-1:00) — show it's simple

## If Something Goes Wrong

### Demo Timeout / Lag
**Say:** "Usually this loads instantly. While we wait, let me explain the token logic..."
**Do:** Keep narrating. Never sit in silence. The story keeps them engaged.

### Website Doesn't Load
**Say:** "Let me reset. This is why the backup is critical." 
**Do:** Navigate to screenshot version. "Here's what this generated. You can see the token logic..."

### You Forget Your Point
**Say:** "Let me rephrase that..." 
**Do:** Pause. Collect yourself. Speak clearly. No one notices unless you act flustered.

### Judge Asks a Question You Can't Answer
**Say:** "That's a great question. Here's my thinking on it..." (then give your best answer)
**Do:** Don't say "I don't know." Always pivot to what you *do* know about the problem/solution.

### Someone Interrupts
**Say:** "Let me finish this thought, then I'll take your question."
**Do:** Keep control. You're the expert here.

## Post-Pitch: If You Don't Win

**Remember:** The judges aren't evaluating you. They're evaluating how well you solved the problem. If you don't win, it's because:

1. You didn't show deep brand intelligence (both brands looked similar)
2. You didn't prove SLDS 2 was actually being used (looked custom CSS)
3. The output didn't look professional (looked "AI-made")
4. You spent too much time on the problem (they already know it)
5. Your demo broke and you panicked

If you walked in with conviction, told a coherent story, showed working prototype, explained the thesis clearly, and answered questions thoughtfully — you've already won. The judges will remember you.
