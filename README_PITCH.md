## ✅ Success Metrics

**You've nailed your pitch if:**
- ✅ The 30-second problem explanation is crystal clear
- ✅ Your approach (SLDS 2 + tokens) is easy to grasp
- ✅ The second brand demo looks completely different from the first
- ✅ When you edit a token, the whole site updates instantly
- ✅ Judges ask "can I use this?" (best compliment)
- ✅ Your closer lands emotionally
- ✅ You answer a curveball question confidently

**Red Flags (Fix These):**
- ❌ Your demo looks generic or AI-like (fix: emphasize uniqueness)
- ❌ Second brand looks too similar to first (fix: pick more distinct personas)
- ❌ You spend >2 min on "what's wrong" (fix: move faster to your solution)
- ❌ Token editing lags or doesn't work (fix: test 10x before)
- ❌ You sound uncertain about SLDS 2 (fix: own this choice)
- ❌ You don't mention "how did it know that?" (fix: save it for closer)

## 📞 If You Get Stuck

### "How do I explain SLDS 2 simply?"
**Say:** "It's the design system that powers Salesforce globally. We apply it with brand intelligence. Think of it like: same vocabulary, different voice."

### "What if judges don't know SLDS 2?"
**Say:** "It's a proven, production-ready design system. The key is: we're not inventing design from scratch. We're applying battle-tested components intelligently."

### "How do I prove Brand Intelligence?"
**Show:** Two brands with same color palette looking completely different. That's the proof.

### "What if the demo breaks?"
**Pivot:** Show screenshot. "Here's what this generated. The logic is..." Keep narrating.

### "How do I handle 'what's your differentiator?'"
**Say:** "We understand personality, not just color. We use a proven system, not custom CSS. And humans stay in control. No other tool does all three."

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Practice the demo more than the pitch.** You need to be so familiar with the flow that you can talk smoothly without watching your screen.

2. **Have backup screenshots.** If demo fails, you can narrate from screenshots. Judges won't know the difference if you're smooth.

3. **Pause after big moments.** After showing the second brand, let silence sit for 2 seconds. After your closer, pause. Silence is powerful.

4. **Make eye contact when closing.** The closer deserves your full presence. Look at the judges. Own it.

5. **Answer questions as extensions of your narrative.** If asked about scalability, tie it back to SLDS 2 + tokens. Keep reinforcing the thesis.

6. **Never apologize for anything.** No "sorry for the typo" or "sorry the demo is slow." Just keep moving.

7. **Confidence > perfection.** A confident, coherent pitch beats a perfect but nervous one every time.
