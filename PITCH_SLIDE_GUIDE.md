## Design Tips for Your Actual Slide

### Color & Contrast
- **Background:** Dark (dark blue or charcoal — matches Salesforce/hackathon vibe)
- **Text:** White or light gray for contrast
- **Accents:** One brand color (the primary you're using in BrandMind AI)
- **Avoid:** Too many colors (let the demo do the work)

### Typography
- **Title:** Large, bold, minimal (40-50pt)
- **Body:** Clean, readable (24-28pt)
- **Emphasis:** Bold or color for key phrases
- **Avoid:** More than 2 fonts (looks unprofessional)

### Visual Elements
- **Left:** Screenshot (1/4 width) showing generic output
- **Center:** Simple flow diagram (1/3 width) — make it clean, not cluttered
- **Right:** 3 short bullet points (1/4 width) — punchy, not paragraphs
- **Bottom:** Your closer as a callout box or highlighted quote

### What NOT to Put on the Slide
- ❌ Too much text (people will read instead of listen)
- ❌ Fake metrics ("87% faster") without proof
- ❌ Jargon without explanation
- ❌ "AI-powered" as your differentiator (everyone says this)
- ❌ Every feature (show 3-5 core benefits, not 20)

**Say:** "Let me show you what's broken today and how we fix it."

### Walk Through (Left Section)
**Say:** "Three things are broken in every AI website builder. First, they read surface signals. See 'coffee,' return brown. Second, there's no system backbone — it's CSS spaghetti. Third, the human has no control."

### Walk Through (Center Section)
**Say:** "We solve this by using SLDS 2 — a battle-tested design system — and mapping brand personality to design tokens. Think of tokens as: if this brand is minimalist, here's what everything should feel like. Same components, completely different personality."

### Walk Through (Right Section)
**Say:** "Why this matters. One: brand intelligence — we tell two brands apart, not just by color. Two: system foundation — SLDS 2 scales. Third: humans stay in control. Every choice is theirs."

### Close (Bottom callout)
**Say (with confidence):** "Every tool generates a website. We generate their website. By the time they see this, they're asking: how did it know that? That's the product."

**Transition to demo:** "Let me show you."
